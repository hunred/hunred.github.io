One year ago, world famous rapper Kendrick Lamar released the album, DAMN. These are the fourteen tracks you find on that record. The main message Kendrick Lamar raps about is God’s punishment, which can be heard in lyrics such as, “Why God, why God do I gotta suffer?” (From FEAR.) “I got dark, I got evil, that rot inside my DNA” (From DNA.) “Love's gonna get you killed, but pride’s gonna be the death of you and you and me” (From PRIDE.) An article from NPR suggests, “His struggles serve as proxy for the human condition, a mirror image of America's own dark soul.” This theme of suffering can be further proved with a voice clip found on the track FEAR, “We are the Israelites, according to the Bible, the children of Israel. He's gonna punish us for our iniquities, for our disobedience, because we chose to follow other gods. That man chasten his son, so the Lord, thy God, chasten thee. So, just like you chasten your own son, He's gonna chastise you because He loves you” A quote from The Badger Herald reveals, “Lamar drives through the album while discussing how modern life is shaped by these predetermined forces.” Kendrick Lamar also provides some moral advice to society, The last song opens with these words, “It was always me versus the world, until I found it's me versus me” In an interview, he says this about his album, “DAMN would be the idea, 'I can't change the world until I change myself’ ... I would hope the listener can take heed ... and carry out their day to the best potential of themself.” Kendrick Lamar also writes a letter explaining his album, saying, “So in conclusion, I feel it's my calling to share the joy of God, but with exclamation, more so, the FEAR OF GOD. The balance. Knowing the power in what he can build, and also what he can destroy. At any given moment.” To help you connect these ethical arguments, I made a poem using all the track titles, summarizing the whole album. But first, you should know that YAH is short for YAHWEH, which is God’s name.
